About Zipf's Law

Zipf's law is a statistical law about word frequency. In almost every language, including English, there is an easily predictable pattern. Take, for example, the word "a." "A" is the third most frequently used word in English. The rank on the list determines its frequency, according to Zipf's law. Since "a" is the third most frequent word in the English language, it is used about 1/3 of the times the most frequent word, "the," is used. "To," the fourth most frequent word, is used 1/4 of the times, etc.
